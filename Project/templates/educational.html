School Trips

Learn about our animals

At RZA, we want people to be able to enjoy their time visiting our animals and have it be something they remember. We love to teach people new things about our animals and share interesting facts so people can always say that they've gained knowledge from us. Making children aware of problems such as deforestation at a younger age makes them more likely to retain that information into adulthood where they can be the ones to combat these issues. This is why we now provide special Educational visit opportunities for schools! We would love for you to visit us and learn all about the animals we home so people can be made aware of the growing global issues regarding the destruction of animal habitats and the endangering of species. Organising a school trip to the RZA means that you are directly supporting our efforts to help animals in need and spreading awareness to arising global issues such as deforestation.

What we offer


Guided Tours

See everything we have to offer







Our trained tour guides will take you through our zoo to ensure you are able to see everything on your stay. They will guide you through the zoo and keep everyone on track with regular headcounts to ensure no one is lost. Pupils will be able to vote on which areas to go to next and if they would like to revisit any. They will be as interactive, helpful, cheerful and informative as they can to give the best experience possible!

Informative talks

Done by trained professionals







Here at RZA, we believe that knowledge is power. This is why we vigorously train our staff to know everything they can on our animals to be able to answer any questions someone may have. They will guide you through the Zoo, taking you to different locations and talk about the animals, their natural habitat, the animals personal story etc. in an informative and digestible manner. These talks will be available and suitable for people of all ages so everyone can enjoy learning!

Animal Interactions

Feeding & petting animals







We also offer pupils the ability to interact directly with some of our animals. With the help of our trained staff, they can hold, pet and feed the animals as well as play with them. We offer up close and personal interactions with a wide variety of our animals, all you have to do is ask! Visitors playing with animals will always be accompanied by one of our professionally trained staff to ensure the safety of the animal as well as the visitor.

Lunch Breaks

For all-day visitors







During your visit, we provide a lunch break where food is provided for attendants. Pupils will have the choice from menu items such as burgers and hot dogs, as well as simpler items such as different sandwiches. During this time, pupils will be seated in a dedicated outdoor lunch area where they can view animals from a distance while eating. We provide you with picnic blankets and benches as to ensure there is more than enough space for everyone to enjoy their food.
