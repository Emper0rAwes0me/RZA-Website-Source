Our Wonderful Zoo Animals

Made possible by you!

Over the years, we have dedicated ourselves to bringing the animals we take in a second chance at life. We hope to bring happiness to these animals while also giving people the chance to experience the sight of these majestic creatures. As we expand, we hope to continue to help out and home these animals in a safe environment where they can flourish in a habitat that suits them. It is from our visitors continued support that we are able to provide the aid that these animals require, and as such we hope to repay the favor by giving you the best experience we can possibly offer!

Our animal Kingdom is vast and as a result we cannot list everyone here. However, we are continually working to expand this list to be as comprehensive as possible to our visitors know what to expect. This does mean you may see some unexpected inhabitants beyond your wildest dreams on your trip!

Incomes Tiffany

Our most famous Tiger







Tiffany has been with us since 2016 when they were just a little kitten. She has grown to be a very independent yet lovable member of our family. She likes to bathe in her waterfall when it gets hot outside to stay cool and loves to chase around her big barrels and other toys! Tiffany can usually be seen laying in her pool under her waterfall or playing with her toys during the afternoon during summer and is well known for always being seen soggy despite her feline nature.

Meet Swanson

One of our many beautiful Tigers







Swanson is a senior Tiger - though his looks may be deceiving - having been found in the wild in 2008 with injuries to his front left leg. As a result, he spends a lot of his time lounging around and relaxing in his own habitat. Swanson is very distinguishable from our other tigers due to his very brightly coloured orange coat despite his older age. When he does decide to move about, usually he does so in a slower manner as his leg cannot support all of his weight. Regardless of his injuries, we still love him and we hope you do to!

Tracey & Paul

Mother & Son duo







These two fur-balls were brought in after the loss of their natural home due to deforestation. They are some of the newest additions to our family, only being brought to us towards the end of 2023. Together, these two wonderful characters spend a lot of their time climbing trees, eating bamboo as well as relaxing in the tall grass in their enclosures, constantly sticking by each other's side. They are a fan favorite as you can commonly them clumsily loafing around!

James

The Playful Panda







James is by far our best crowd pleaser. James came to us with Tracey and Paul as the lone survivor of his family. Despite his shortcomings and the loss of his family, he never fails to put a smile on his visitors faces. Commonly, you can see him pulling funny faces or laying in odd positions - anything that could get people to chuckle! He loves to roll about his enclosure as well as lay back and relax. We have yet to see a disappointed visitor after they meet James.

Kai

The Crazy Koala







Sometimes it may be a struggle to get a good look at Kai due to his active nature. He loves to zip about his enclosure, jumping up and between the many trees he has access to. He likes to stay active and rarely slows down, so you need a keen eye to keep up! When he isn't rampaging about he can most often be found in the taller parts of trees resting where he is out of the way.

Toby

The lazy Koala







Toby is the polar opposite of Kai. It is rare you will see this lethargic boy leave his favorite hiding spot in one of their trees. Toby loves to sit and munch on the leaves of trees all day and watch as Kai zooms about. Sometimes Kai will come to say hello when he needs a rest and they will snack together! He only tends to move when he has run out of reachable food sources, so keep an eye out for when he does!
